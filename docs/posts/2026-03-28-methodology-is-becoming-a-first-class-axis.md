# Methodology is becoming a first-class axis

For a while, the AI ecosystem looked like it could be mapped mostly through tools.

Then it became clear that tools were not enough.
We needed to map:
- runtimes
- harnesses
- workflows
- capability layers
- interface layers

Now another shift is becoming visible:

> **methodology itself is becoming a first-class axis.**

This means the important question is no longer only:
- what agent are you using?
- what harness are you using?

It is increasingly also:
- **what method are you using to work with AI?**

---

## What this means
A methodology is more than a prompt.
It is more than a tool.
It is more than a model choice.

It is a repeatable way of:
- scoping work
- structuring interaction
- decomposing problems
- storing progress
- evaluating output
- deciding when humans intervene

This is why methodologies like:
- vibe-coding
- harness engineering
- executable SSOT
- spec-driven development
- collaborative learning
- vibe-physics

matter so much.

They are not just trends in language.
They are signals that the ecosystem is stabilizing around ways of working.

---

## Why vibe-physics matters here
Anthropic's **Vibe physics: The AI grad student** is especially useful because it makes the methodology visible.

The story is not:
- “AI autonomously did science.”

The more interesting story is:
- a human expert created a structured process,
- decomposed the work,
- kept the AI inside strict operating rules,
- organized artifacts into a retrieval-friendly task tree,
- and used domain expertise to supervise quality.

That is a method.

And it rhymes with many other signals we have already been tracking.

---

## The broader pattern
The same methodological turn appears in multiple places:

### Harness engineering
Not just using an agent, but giving it a reusable operating environment.

### Executable SSOT
Not just documenting a workflow, but encoding it so humans and agents can both act from it.

### Agent patterns
Not just coding with AI, but choosing architectural patterns like supervisor, orchestrator-subagent, evaluator-optimizer, HITL.

### Voice / talk mode
Not just adding speech, but designing a realtime interaction method with latency, interruption, and multilingual reliability.

### Vibe-physics
Not just applying AI to a domain, but inventing a staged way for humans and AI to do domain work together.

---

## Why clawfit needs this axis
If clawfit only tracks tools, it will miss too much.

Two teams can use the same runtime, the same model, and the same harness and still perform very differently because their **methodology** is different.

That means methodology is not soft context.
It is part of the system.

So clawfit increasingly needs to ask:
- what runtimes are people using?
- what harness layers are they adding?
- what capabilities are they extending?
- what interfaces are they operating through?
- and **what methodology is actually driving the work?**

---

## Working conclusion
The next layer of maturity in the AI ecosystem may not be a better model or a new wrapper.
It may be a clearer language for **how humans and AI work together**.

That is why methodology is becoming a first-class axis.
